Deep Dive Coding: Java + Android(2020), Fullstack Web (2021)

I completed Deep Dive Java + Android in April of 2020, and I will finish the Fullstack bootcamp in June 2021. Coding connects a lot of the interests that drive my hobbies, so I picked up the basics of Java very quickly. I pushed myself with independent practice, and within a few weeks I was helping my classmates when our instructors were busy.

I built a web-scraping recipe app I named Choppit as a solo project, and had just started working in a team on Scavengr, an AR scavenger hunt app, when COVID-19 gave us all a crash course in remote work. I led my team in daily check-ins and planning calls, and managed our workflow by creating an Asana project to organize ideas and assign tasks. We leaned hard into the use of GitHub branches and learned as we worked. Without our instructors in the next room to answer questions, we did a lot of independent research to fix bugs and work out implementations.

Thanks to Deep Dive Java + Android, I am proficient with IntelliJ IDE and Java SE 8+. I have experience with JUnit4 and 5 testing , relational database design using MySQL or SQLite, RESTful services in Spring, AndroidX libraries and ViewModel architecture incorporating ReactiveX tasks, third-party libraries, and more. I spent most of the pandemic laying low and splitting my time between studying for the Java 8 OCA exam and developing Choppit. It is currently at minimum viability, and I'm excited to continue with it after completing Deep Dive Fullstack.

The experience of practicing with HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, React, React-Bootstrap, Node.js, etc. has been a very different kind of challenge than learning exclusively Java. There is a lot of skill overlap, but also a new set of challenges in functional programming. I still haven't decided how I feel about weak typing in JavaScript. The Fullstack bootcamp has the advantage of being prepared for remote work, so I have also become much more familiar with Asana, Slack and Git/GitHub.

My next project after completing Deep Dive Fullstack will be to take the core Java of Choppit, add in the kind of Spring endpoints we built for our Scavengr server, and rework the UI in React so that it can be deployed as a standalone web app.

Verizon Wireless

I worked at a Verizon Wireless call center from 2010 to 2018. The majority of my experience is in tech support; the Albuquerque center was among the first to support complex services like international roaming, home/mobile internet devices, and network extenders (micro cell towers that connect to the Verizon network through a wired internet connection) on top of all the changes in cell phone technology over the last decade. I saw each of these as an opportunity to develop expertise with each new set of procedures, challenges and work-arounds as fast as possible.

I got my A+ certification to move up in Tech Support, and my Network+ because it looked cool. I prided myself on the ability to guide even the least confident and most tech-phobic customers through their router admin screens to configure DHCP reservations, port forwarding, occasionally even QoS and DMZ settings - without being able to see their computer screen. Through these experiences, I became highly skilled at applying research, documentation and critical thinking to problems. I also perfected the ability to communicate between laypersons and technical experts.

In my last four years at Verizon I went through leadership training and worked as a Supervisor - either officially or ad hoc - for most of that time. I also used Verizon’s tuition assistance program to earn a Master’s in Adult Education, with an emphasis in professional learning and development. I discovered a surprisingly deep overlap between teaching and leadership, which helped me motivate my teams to work harder and smarter, taking pride and initiative in their personal and professional growth. The ADDIE training model* guided me in creating and iteratively improving learner-focused training modules for everything from resolving usage disputes to building trust.
*Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation.
